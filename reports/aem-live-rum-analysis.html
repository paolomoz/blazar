      Methodology

      How this report was produced and what its limitations are.

      Data Sources

      • RUM bundles: Fetched from bundles.aem.page for www.aem.live, covering Feb 15–24, 2026 (10 days). 1,648 raw bundles processed.
      • Static analysis: data/aem-live/analysis.json generated by scripts/aem-live-content-gaps.mjs on Feb 24, 2026. Covers 197 indexed pages, 192 sitemap URLs.
      • Processing script: scripts/aem-live-rum-analysis.mjs cross-references both sources, outputs data/aem-live/rum-feb-2026.json.

      Sampling & Weighting

      • AEM RUM uses 1:100 sampling for production traffic (each observed event represents ~100 real events).
      • Preview/Sidekick traffic uses 1:10 sampling.
      • All counts in this report are weighted (pre-multiplied by sampling weight).
      • For pages with <300 estimated views, the data represents a single observation — treat these as directional, not precise.

      Limitations

      • 10-day window: Not a full month. Traffic patterns may differ across the full month (e.g., beginning-of-month vs. end-of-month).
      • Bot traffic: ~1% (1,700 views) is classified as bot traffic. Not filtered because bot classification is approximate and the volume is low.
      • CWV sampling noise: Pages with few CWV samples (1–3) may show extreme values. The homepage (84 TTFB samples) is highly reliable; single-sample pages are indicative only.
      • External referrers: Below the sampling threshold for this period. The data captures internal navigation patterns well but underrepresents external traffic sources like Google Search, LinkedIn, or direct bookmarks.
      • Priority assignments: The shift matrix applies human judgment to traffic signals. Reasonable people could assign different priorities — the traffic data itself is objective, the interpretation is not.
