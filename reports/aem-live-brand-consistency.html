Systemic Findings

Patterns that emerge across multiple pages, revealing structural voice governance issues rather than individual page problems.

Voice Gradient: Developer Pages vs. Docs Pages

Pages under /developer/ average 82.5 brand alignment. Pages under /docs/ average 72.7. The gap suggests the developer docs were written by the core engineering team (the brand's original voice), while docs pages had more contributors with less voice training.

  • PATTERN: /developer/ pages show consistently higher Declarative Confidence and Controlled Playfulness.
  • PATTERN: /docs/ pages show higher Inclusive Directness but lower Engineering Pragmatism -- softer voice for authors.
  • PATTERN: /business/ pages are a completely different voice -- pure marketing, zero engineering DNA.

The "Playfulness Cliff"

Controlled Playfulness is the most variable trait across the site, ranging from 4/5 (tutorial, keeping-it-100) to 1/5 (FAQ, RUM, custom-headers, SharePoint setup). The brand's dry humor is concentrated in a few flagship pages and absent from the long tail of documentation.

  • PATTERN: 6 out of 12 pages score 1/5 or 2/5 on Controlled Playfulness.
  • PATTERN: Pages that score high on playfulness also score high overall -- it is a strong predictor of brand alignment.

The FAQ Problem

The FAQ is the highest-traffic reference page and the second-lowest scoring page (58). Its repetitive "Yes, Edge Delivery Services..." pattern, generic "great solution" language, and absence of opinion make it the single largest brand consistency risk because of its visibility.

  • CRITICAL: High traffic + low brand alignment = the FAQ is likely many users' first and most lasting impression of the brand voice.
  • ROOT CAUSE: FAQ was likely assembled incrementally from support tickets rather than authored with voice governance.

Performance Moralism Distribution

Performance Moralism -- the trait that most distinguishes AEM from competitors -- scores 5/5 on the homepage and keeping-it-100 but drops to 1/5 on 4 pages (custom-headers, SharePoint setup, business/demo, and partially on RUM). The brand's defining trait is inconsistently applied.

  • GAP: Setup guides, reference pages, and API docs rarely connect their content to performance outcomes.
  • OPPORTUNITY: Adding a "performance impact" note to every docs page would reinforce the brand's core value.
